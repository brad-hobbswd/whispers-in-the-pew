Stand Up and Be Counted

Luke 12:8

Scripture:

“And I tell you, whoever declares openly and confesses that he is My worshiper and acknowledges Me before men, the Son of Man also will declare and confess and acknowledge him before the angels of God.” Luke 12:8

Devotion:

Pressure to blend in shows up in quiet ways. Conversations shift. Opportunities to speak pass by. Moments that call for boldness become moments of hesitation.

Jesus calls His people to something different. A life that is not hidden, not divided, and not shaped by fear.

Confessing Him goes beyond words spoken in church settings. A life aligned with Him becomes the confession. Choices, responses, integrity, and love all reveal where the heart stands.

Courage does not always look loud. Many times, courage looks steady. Choosing truth when silence feels safer. Living consistently when compromise would be easier. Standing firm when pressure rises.

Jesus gave a promise that carries eternal weight. Those who acknowledge Him now will be acknowledged by Him before the angels. That kind of honor reaches far beyond anything this world offers.

Faith was never meant to be hidden. Faith was meant to be lived.

Reflection:

• Does your life reflect your faith in every environment?
• Where have you felt pressure to stay silent?
• What would courage look like in your current season?

Prayer:

Lord, strengthen my heart to live openly for You. Give me courage to stand in truth with love and integrity. Help me remain steady when pressure rises and faithful in every place I walk. Let my life reflect who You are. In Jesus’ name, amen.
